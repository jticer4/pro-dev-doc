My Reflections:

Week 0: My first week here at Deep Dive has been amazing! It's so nice to get to know everybody and see all of the different personalities. This week started out with some review on creating and deploying to repositories. We then learned about creating personas and use/case interaction flows. It was really fun to work in groups while designing our personas. We then moved on to databases and learned about their entities, and their corresponding attributes. We then learned about Entity Relationship Diagrams, foreign/primary keys and some commands in mySQL.

Week 1: My second week here at Deep Dive has been a blast! It seems like things have really started to pick up pace, however, although it may be challenging, it has been just as rewarding. This week we chose photos to represent how we see ourselves now, and how we see ourselves two years in the future. It was really cool to visualize where we want to be, and take a chance to think about it's going to feel like. The picture I chose to represent myself currently was a photo of a man being blasted from underneath with a massive water fountain. I felt this represented me because of the massive amount of information that I've been absorbing. The image I chose to represent myself in the future is of a cat sitting comfortably in a chair confidently analyzing a situation. I felt like this represents me in the future because the cat is calm, cool, collected, and most importantly not being blasted with a massive water fountain. This week we also began learning Object Oriented PHP, more specifically about classes, getter and setter methods, traits, constructor methods, and composer.json files. It has been a lot of material but after reading a ton of documentation, and getting some practice under my belt, I feel like I'm starting to learn the basics. Another one of our assignments this week was to research our web presence on Google. We were instructed to find our online identity, channels, and the content within those channels. From this exercise I discovered that my web presence is shared equally with my father as we share the same name. The same profiles come up, LinkedIn, FaceBook, Ancestry.com. However there is a blank profile for James Ticer that comes up under my dad's but it's not mine. This is definitely something I should look into and fix sooner rather than later.
